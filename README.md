# Herman (Zvi) Freiman — Holocaust Survival Research

This repository documents genealogical and historical research into the life of **Herman Zvi Freiman (צבי פריימן)**, a Polish Jewish Holocaust survivor born in Boryslaw, Galicia (now Ukraine) on March 2, 1910. He survived the Boryslaw Ghetto and Forced Labor Camp (ZAL), emigrated to Israel in 1948, and lived in Kfar Ata. His first wife and children perished in the Holocaust.

## Research Report Highlights

The [main report](Herman_Freiman_Research_Report.md) consolidates documents from three independent sources into a comprehensive record covering:

- **Biographical data** — identity, profession, family
- **Wartime timeline** — Boryslaw Ghetto and ZAL, 1941–1944
- **Post-war journey** — Poland, DP Camp Ulm (1946–1948), immigration to Israel on the ship *Negba*
- **Genealogical family tree** — parents, two marriages, three sons
- **German restitution** — Wiedergutmachung file E.A. Stuttgart 11 672(o)
- **Document transcriptions** — German and Polish originals with English translations
- **Open research leads** — archives and next steps for filling remaining gaps
